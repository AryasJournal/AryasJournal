Researching the Electrical Side of Our Stardome Project

By Arya Lekshmi S • AstroCET • College of Engineering Trivandrum

Every engineering project begins with research before implementation. As part of the AstroCET Stardome Project, our team's responsibility is the Electrical Subsystem. Before selecting components or designing circuits, I started by understanding how a Stardome works and what makes a projection system efficient, reliable, and suitable for astronomy education.

Understanding the Stardome

The first step was understanding the working principle of a portable planetarium. I studied how light is generated, how it travels through optical components, and how thousands of stars are projected onto the dome surface. Learning the complete system helped me understand how different engineering subsystems work together.

Research on Light Sources

One of my main research areas was the selection of the light source. I compared different LED technologies, including standard LEDs, high-power LEDs, and COB LEDs. During this study, I analysed brightness, luminous efficiency, colour temperature, power consumption, lifespan, and heat generation. Instead of selecting a component based only on brightness, I learned that every specification contributes to the overall projection quality.

Thermal Management

High-power LEDs generate heat, making thermal management an important consideration. I explored different cooling methods, including heat sinks, thermal interface materials, and airflow techniques, to understand how they improve efficiency and increase component lifespan.

Learning Beyond the Electrical Subsystem

Although my primary focus is the electrical subsystem, I also researched optical projection, lens systems, beam angles, and the arrangement of components inside professional planetarium projectors. Studying technical figures and existing designs helped me understand how electrical and optical engineering work together.

What I Learned

This research has shown me that engineering is much more than assembling hardware. Every design decision begins with understanding the problem, comparing available technologies, and selecting the most suitable solution based on technical requirements. The Stardome project is currently in progress, and this research forms the foundation for the electrical subsystem that our team will continue to develop.

"Engineering starts with curiosity. Every paper read, every figure analysed, and every comparison made brings us one step closer to building a better system."
