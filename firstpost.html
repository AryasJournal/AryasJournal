Why I Started Arya's Journal

June 2026

Hello and welcome to the very first page of Arya's Journal.

I'm Arya, an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at the College of Engineering Trivandrum. If there is one thing that describes me best, it is curiosity. I have always been fascinated by how things work. Whether it is a small LED, a motor, a computer program, or a complex engineering system, I find myself constantly asking questions and looking for answers.

One thing I have noticed about myself is that I love documenting things. Whenever I learn something interesting, discover a new concept, or come across an exciting idea, my first instinct is to write it down. My notebooks are filled with observations, project ideas, questions, and random thoughts that I don't want to forget.

As I started my engineering journey, I realized that every day introduced me to something new. One day I would be learning about circuits, another day about programming, and the next day about LEDs, semiconductors, or space technology. Every topic opened the door to more questions, and every answer seemed to lead to another area waiting to be explored.

That is how the idea for Arya's Journal was born. I wanted a place where I could record my journey, not only the achievements but also the lessons, experiments, challenges, and moments of curiosity that shape who I am becoming as an engineer.

This journal is not meant to be a perfect portfolio or a collection of polished success stories. Instead, it is a record of growth. A place where I can share what I learn, what I build, what inspires me, and sometimes even what goes wrong. After all, engineering is not just about finding answers; it is about asking questions and learning through the process.

Through this journal, I hope to document my experiences at CET, my involvement in communities like AstroCET and IEDC CET, the projects I work on, and the ideas that keep me curious. Years from now, I hope to look back at these entries and see how much I have grown, how my interests evolved, and how a simple habit of writing things down turned into a meaningful journey.

If you are reading this, thank you for being here. I hope you enjoy following along as I learn, build, explore, and document the adventure one entry at a time.

— Arya 🌿
